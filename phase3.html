Phase 3: Extraction & Exfiltration

At precisely H+45 minutes (0315 hours), the Indian Armed Forces initiated the final and most delicate phase of Operation Sindoor: the coordinated extraction and exfiltration of all deployed personnel. Following the successful kinetic and cyber assaults, maintaining stealth, safety, and speed became paramount.

Across hostile terrain, Garud Commandos sprang into action to secure temporary Landing Zones (LZs), deploying infrared strobes and laser beacons to guide NVG-equipped Mi-17V5 helicopters from the elite 109 Helicopter Unit. Each LZ was secured under tight defensive perimeters, covered by MANPADS teams and sniper overwatch. Simultaneously, MARCOS teams operating deep behind enemy lines began their covert maritime withdrawal toward rendezvous points along the Arabian Sea.

Under the ocean’s veil, INS Shankul — an HDW-class submarine — silently surfaced to extract MARCOS divers, while fast attack boats equipped with low-RCS hulls escorted remaining units through the narrow creeks and river mouths, tracing a path from Sir Creek to the Gujarat coast.

Overhead, the skies were alive with persistent surveillance. Heron Mk-II UAVs hovered quietly, providing real-time ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance & Reconnaissance) and overwatch on troop movement and enemy positioning. Netra AEW&C aircraft ensured early detection of any air threats, while Su-30MKIs equipped with EL/M-20600 EW pods jammed enemy radar and communication signals throughout the corridor.

The last of the troops cleared the operational boundary by 0433 hours. With the final signal received from Command HQ, Phase 3 was officially marked as “COMPLETE”. All 124 special operations personnel returned to base safely — a testament to the flawless coordination, technological superiority, and bravery of India’s elite forces.
